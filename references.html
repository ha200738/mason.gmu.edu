References

Brynjolfsson, E., & McAfee, A. (2014). The Second Machine Age: Work, Progress, and Prosperity in a Time of Brilliant Technologies. W. W. Norton & Company.

 

Brynjolfsson and McAfee talk on how modern technology, especially artificial intelligence (AI), affects work, productivity, and economic growth, among other areas of society. The book explores how breakthroughs led by AI are changing businesses and impacting people. Even if the findings are not specifically related to healthcare, they offer an overview for understanding how AI could change fields like healthcare by increasing productivity. The authors also talk about concerns such as potential job losses and the need to change policies. Brynjolfsson and McAfee are experts in technology and economics and the book is frequently referenced in scholarly and business debates over the possibilities of AI. 

 

Esteva, A., Robicquet, A., Ramsundar, B., Kuleshov, V., DePristo, M., Chou, K., Cui, C., Corrado, G., Thrun, S., & Dean, J. (2018). A guide to deep learning in healthcare. Nature Medicine, 25(1), 24–29. https://doi.org/10.1038/s41591-018-0316-z

 

This article offers a thorough summary of the uses of artificial intelligence in the medical field. The article talks about the variety of uses in healthcare, including imaging, diagnostics, and disease detection, highlighting how AI may be more effective than traditional methods. The authors describe the legal and technical obstacles to the usage of AI. Because it was published in Nature Medicine, an established peer-reviewed journal, the article is an accurate source that combines scientific credibility with practical information. In clinical settings, it is especially helpful for understanding the technical advantages and disadvantages of AI, making it an important tool for healthcare AI research.

 

Cabuyao, K. (2023, October 6). Artificial intelligence and cybersecurity in healthcare (YEL2023) - IHF. IHF. https://ihf-fih.org/news-insights/artificial-intelligence-and-cybersecurity-in-healthcare/

 

The article by Cabuyao explores the relationship between cybersecurity and artificial intelligence in the healthcare industry, highlighting the risks that AI systems pose to the privacy of patient data. She talks about the need for strong security measures for protecting private patient data and the growing complexity of threats that target healthcare data. As healthcare institutions grow more digitally, the article emphasizes how crucial it is to add a balance between security concerns and technological advancement. The text is trustworthy and important to comprehending security issues because it was published by the International Hospital Federation (IHF), a respectable organization in the field of global health. It offers helpful background information on the dangers of incorporating AI in healthcare as well as the steps required to lessen these risks.

 

Obermeyer, Z., Powers, B., Vogeli, C., & Mullainathan, S. (2019). Dissecting racial bias in an algorithm used to manage the health of populations. Science, 366(6464), 447–453. https://doi.org/10.1126/science.aax2342

This study highlights a serious ethical concern with AI applications by looking at racial bias in healthcare systems. In their study, they look at a popular health management method that unintentionally gives preference to white patients over Black patients, resulting in disparities in treatment. The study highlights how biases in training data can lead to unfair outcomes and highlights the significance of developing artificial intelligence that is more fair. The work is trustworthy and provides important insights into the ethical issues surrounding AI in healthcare. The information provided is crucial for understanding the possible dangers of bias in algorithms and highlights the necessity of developing AI fairly and openly.

 

Education, I. (2024b, September 3). AI healthcare benefits. IBM. https://www.ibm.com/think/insights/ai-healthcare-benefits

 

This IBM article gives a summary of the advantages AI offers the healthcare industry, such as improved efficiency, individualized treatment, and accurate diagnosis. The article emphasizes Watson Health, one of IBM's AI tools that helps physicians evaluate data and make decisions. The site is useful since it offers insights into actual uses of AI and describes advantages for healthcare systems, despite its promotional tone. Given that IBM is a well-known technological business with experience in AI development, the information is trustworthy for understanding the practical benefits of AI. This source is useful to learn more about how AI might help healthcare professionals improve patient outcomes.

 

Big data analytics in healthcare − A systematic literature review and roadmap for practical implementation. (2021, January 1). IEEE Journals & Magazine | IEEE Xplore. https://ieeexplore.ieee.org/document/9205683

This source analyzes the current situation of big data analytics in the medical field, which serves as the core of many applications for artificial intelligence. In order to predict patient outcomes, improve treatments, and further improve medical procedures, the article explains how AI uses large amounts of data. Data standards, integration, and privacy issues are among the practical challenges of putting big data analytics into effect. The article offers a thorough understanding of the practical and technical challenges surrounding AI in healthcare. When it comes to understanding the systems required to support AI and big data applications in healthcare settings, this source is especially beneficial.

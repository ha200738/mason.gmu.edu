Artificial Intelligence in Healthcare

Huria Ahmad

Security & Social Concerns

The Threats AI Poses to Cybersecurity of Healthcare Systems Cybercriminals tend to target healthcare since medical data is very valuable. Data shows how the increase in artificial intelligence can inadvertently expose health systems to short-term vulnerability from hackers. (Cabuyao, 2023). The hacking would endanger patient safety and severely undermine confidence in AI’s role in health care. Socially, the use of AI in healthcare can mean lesser human interaction as certain tasks might be computerized. Because it affects many patients who derive satisfaction from their healthcare professionals, this could have implications for patient satisfaction and quality of service. Another social issue is the potential job losses for health workers. AI may not take over all healthcare fields, but some jobs have an increased risk—particularly administration and diagnostics where it can be more computerized.

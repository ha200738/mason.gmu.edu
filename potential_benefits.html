Potential Benefits

There are various reasons to be hopeful about AI in healthcare, such as facilitating treatment planning and enhancing diagnosis accuracy. For areas such as radiology and pathology, we also note a potential for better diagnosis by a machine learning algorithm that allows illnesses to be detected earlier. (Esteva et al. 2019) describes how machine learning algorithms trained on massive datasets can accurately identify skin cancer at rates comparable to dermatologists and argues thatthat AI may be used in the future to propel science forward by ensuring the accuracy of our medical knowledge. Administrative work is also likely to be replaced by AI, which will help increase hospital productivity and give adequate time for medical staff to visit patients. (BM Education, 2024). AI-driven predictive analytics also identifies high-risk patients potentially developing chronic diseases, as it enables immediate remedial actions, thus saving costs associated with health care. (Imran et al., 2021).
